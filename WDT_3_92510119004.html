Linus Torvalds
1969 - Present
Creator of Linux and Git
Linus Benedict Torvalds (born December 28, 1969) is a Finnish-American software engineer who is the creator and, historically, the main developer of the Linux kernel. He also created the distributed version control system Git.
Linus Torvalds, the man behind the Linux kernel.
The following list is a timeline of Linus Torvalds' Life
1969 - Born in Helsinki, Finland.
1981 - Began programming in BASIC at age 11 on a Commodore VIC-20.
1988 - Enrolled at the University of Helsinki, majoring in computer science.
1991 - Purchased an IBM-compatible PC and began developing a UNIX-like operating system; the first Linux prototypes were publicly released.
1994 - Linux Version 1.0 was officially released on March 14.
1996 - Completed his M.Sc. thesis, titled "Linux: A Portable Operating System."
1997 - Moved to California to work for Transmeta Corporation.
2003 - Left Transmeta to focus exclusively on Linux kernel development at OSDL (now The Linux Foundation).
2005 - Created Git, the world's most popular distributed version control system.
2012 - Inducted into the Internet Hall of Fame and awarded the Millennium Technology Prize.
2024 - Continues to lead Linux development as a Fellow at the Linux Foundation.
